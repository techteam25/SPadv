Story Tell-Back

 

Who:  One or two bilingual people, who know English (or French or Spanish or common language with the Accuracy Checker) will hold the phone and do the work for this phase.  It is best if these back translators were not involved in the translation of the story, nor involved in the community checks.

Purpose:  The purpose of the Story Tell Back phase is to send an audio retelling in the source language of the entire local language story draft translation to a Remote Oral Consultant Checker working on the ROCC web app.  Telling back a summary of the story should help the Checker to discern how accurately and meaningfully the story was translated into the local language.  The whole story tell back will compliment the slide by slide tell back (back translation) and give the Accuracy Checker a high level overview version of the story to compare to the individual slides’ phrase by phrase back translations.

 

Activities:

  1. The back translator(s) should play the video story several times in the local language, listening and watching, until they have the translated story well in their mind. Tap the triangle play button under the image to play the video story.  Press and slide the dot on the scrub bar to move backward or forward in the story.  If you wish to practise retelling the story while looking at the pictures but without the audio, slide the audio-speaker icon over to the left to mute the sound.

  2. Tap the mic button at the bottom of the screen and retell the story, or a summary of what the story is about, including what it means, in the Gateway language, all in one go.

  3. Stop your recording by tapping the square button.

  4. If you want to record again your story tell back, tap the mic again and say ‘yes’ to overwrite and delete the first recording.  Note:  Summarizing or telling a story back into the Gateway language does not need to be perfect.  This recording will not be published.  Only one audio file is allowed to be recorded in this phase.

  5. Tap the up-arrow-in-the-cloud icon to send the Story Tell Back audio file to the Accuracy Checker / ROCC.  When the phone has connectivity or is online, the file will be sent. A tick mark will replace the arrow in the cloud and the cloud will turn blue when ROCC has received the audio file.

  6. Swipe down to the Slide Tell Back phase or use the drop down menu on the top of the screen to tap to the Slide Tell Back phase.

 

Notes:

●     Only one recording (the last one) will be saved.

●     You will not be allowed to pause this recording.

●     The audio recording is saved separate from the progression of the pictures.  It is ok if the story tell back recording does not coordinate or sync with the pictures.  Only an audio file will be sent to the ROCC.
