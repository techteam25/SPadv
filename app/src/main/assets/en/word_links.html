Word Links

Who: Translators and Accuracy checkers will find this tool or feature helpful to utilize.

Purpose: The purposes of the WordLinks tool are:

  • to provide convenient access to difficult words and names across all the stories

  • to build a database of local language audio renderings for difficult words and names in a single location

  • to assist translators to render Biblical key terms consistently and to pronounce people and place names consistently in/across all the stories

  • to provide literal text back-translations for all the difficult words and names (and transliterations of names) as another means to help an Accuracy Checker in their work.

Activities:

  1. From the sidebar menu, Word Links list:

    1. Type in the search bar to find a specific term or name, or scroll through the alphabetized words to find what you are looking for.

    2. Tap a word/name to open the popup window for that term.

  2. Tap a hyperlinked term from the TRANSLATE or ACCURACY CHECK phase, text section of each slide.

    1. A blue hyperlink indicates that there is no audio rendering for that term. Tapping a blue hyperlink will navigate the user to the research screen for the term.

    2. A clear hyperlink (underlined normal text) indicates that an audio rendering (at least one) has been made for that term. Tapping a clear hyperlink will navigate the user to the audio and text documentation screen for the term. Slide up to see the research screen.

  3. Read and research the meaning of a term. Discuss how to render the term in the context of the story in the local language. Consider other related but different terms.

  4. Document a local language term or word.

    1. Tap the mic button at the bottom of the screen to record an audio translation of the term in the local language. Tap the square button to stop the recording. 

    2. Press and hold on the audio file to type a word-for-word literal back translation in the source language (LWC). Or, if a name was recorded, type a (phonetic) transliteration of the word.

  5. Recall how a word was rendered. Tap a clear hyperlink to open the Word Link popup window, documentation screen. Tap the triangle play button to listen to how a name or term was rendered in another story. Exit the popup window to resume translation work.

  6. Exit the Word Link popup window by tapping the X in the upper right corner of the screen. Resume your translation and checking activities. Or sometimes, tapping the back arrow on your phone will put you back where you just came from.

Notes:

  1. The WordLinks database notes are intended to be non-encyclopedic in nature -- just a few notes to give translators essential and basic meaning or background.  If there is an existing local language Scripture translation or Bible translation team, these should always be consulted so there is consistency in how terms and names are rendered in the stories.

  2. Two types of words are included in the WordLinks database:

    1. Biblical key terms. Some of these terms may have multiple senses depending on their context. Translators may need to record multiple audio renderings for one key term and discern which rendering to use in which context.  Multiple audio renderings might also need to be recorded to document different forms of a term (e.g. verb form and noun form)

    2. Names for people and places. The primary purpose for recording an audio rendering for names is to provide consistency in pronunciation across all the stories.

  1. There are several sections in the WordLinks popup window Research screen to aid the story translator in their decision for how to render the term:

    1. The hyperlinked word from the story followed by its various grammatical forms.  These will be at the top of the popup window in the brown section.

    2. Meaning Notes -- brief definitions or background information.

    3. Related (but different) terms, hyperlinked -- to provide some material for comparison and contrast, further research.

    4. Example renderings from other languages, back translated -- to provide practical ideas for how story translators might want to translate the term in their local language.

  2. The recording bar at the bottom of the screen will expand into an extended screen below the research screen once a recording has been made for a key term.  Once audio renderings have been recorded for a term, they can be reviewed in this extended documentation screen -- the audio rendering in the local language can be listened to and the associated back-translated text can be read. 

  3. The WordLinks local language database could be backed up on a regular basis.  The WordLinks folder is located in the root of the SP Workspace directory (which is maybe stored on the SD card). For backup, copy the WordLinks directory to another drive or card.

  4. Can words can be added to the WordLinks database? Words can be added to the database, but not from within the SPadv app. It is a multi-step process to remove the database, change the csv file back into a xlsx file, add the desired words in the proper column(s), save the database as a csv file, put the csv file back in the SP Workspace/wordlinks directory and then open the SPadv app so that the Workspace is scanned afresh.
